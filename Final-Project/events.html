Thursday, July 10th 2023 Family Abolition

In Family Abolition , author M.E. O'Brien uncovers the history of struggles to create radical alternatives to the private family. From early Marxists to Black and queer insurrectionists to today's mass protest movements, O'Brien finds revolutionaries seeking better ways of loving, caring, and living. "[Family Abolition is] an immensely useful book that will help us not just understand the violence of gender and family relations, but also take action to establish new methods of caring for one another and building survivable social relations."-Dean Spade Join author M.E. O'Brien in conversation with Joseph DeFilippis and Shannon Perez-Darby Drawing on their organizing around the politics of social welfare, DeFilippis and Shannon-Darby will engage how Family Abolition resonates with their own work and thinking. This is an all ages event and masks are required.
